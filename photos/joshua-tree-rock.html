Quartz Monzogranite Monolith
📍 Joshua Tree National Park, California  ·  📷 Fujifilm X-T30 II  ·  March 21, 2025

Massive formations of quartz monzogranite dominate the landscape of Joshua Tree, appearing as though they were stacked by giants. This monolithic boulder, characterized by deep vertical fissures and a golden patina of desert varnish, is the result of millions of years of subterranean cooling followed by surface erosion.
